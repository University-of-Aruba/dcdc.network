The people behind the DCDC Network

Real people, real islands

The DCDC Network is built by researchers, educators, and professionals who live and work in the Dutch Caribbean. We share a belief that open data and collaborative research make the region stronger.

Rendell de Kort

Network Administrator & Facilitator
Aruba · University of Aruba
What I work on
I run an economics consultancy focused on sustainable and purpose-driven business models, and I'm completing a PhD on economic resilience in small island states. Data and how we use it sits at the center of most of what I do.
Why this matters to me
The Dutch Caribbean produces real knowledge. But too much of it stays locked inside organizations, never shared, never built upon. That's a loss we can actually do something about, and that's why I said yes to this role.
What I'm hoping DCDC makes possible
A researcher in St. Martin being able to build on work done in Aruba without starting from scratch. That kind of quiet, practical collaboration is what the region has been missing.

Curaçao

Marjorie Alfonso

Local Network Coordinator
Curaçao · University of Curaçao
What I work on
I work as an Information and AI Specialist at the University of Curaçao Library, where I support researchers and lecturers with research tools, digital resources, and emerging technologies such as AI. My work also focuses on research support, digital scholarship, and helping improve access to academic knowledge and data.
Why this matters to me
Open science and digital competence matter to me because researchers and students on small islands should have the same opportunities to access knowledge, collaborate internationally, and make their work visible. Strengthening digital skills and open research practices can really help our academic communities grow.
What I'm hoping DCDC makes possible
I hope the DCDC Network helps build stronger collaboration between the Caribbean islands and international partners, while creating more opportunities for training, knowledge sharing, and digital innovation in research and education.
